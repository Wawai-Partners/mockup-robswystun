A writer, by training and by habit.

A short version: a published fiction author, book ghostwriter, and B2B content marketer working alone, by hand, from Winnipeg. Twenty years in. Still writing every word himself.

01

The long version, in five paragraphs.

I've been writing professionally since 2005, when I sold my first piece of journalism while still figuring out what professional writing even meant. Twenty years later, I have a clearer answer: it means showing up with the discipline of a tradesperson and the ear of an editor, for whoever is paying you to do it well.

¶ 1 of 5 On entering the trade in 2005, one paragraph at a time.

The ghostwriting practice.

I've been ghostwriting books and long-form thought-leadership for thirteen years. The clients are usually executives, founders, and professionals with a book's worth of expertise but no spare ten months to turn it into a manuscript. My job is to extract what they know, write it in their voice, and disappear from the acknowledgments page. Business books, memoir, the occasional novel.

¶ 2 of 5 — Ghostwriting 13+ years. Business books, memoirs, the occasional novel.

The B2B content marketing practice.

On the marketing side, I've written for 90+ companies — articles, blogs, white papers, case studies, press releases, landing pages. Mostly for B2B teams in SaaS, biosciences, fintech, electric vehicles, and franchising. The work is straightforward: understand the audience, do the research, write something a real person would want to finish reading.

¶ 3 of 5 — B2B writing 90+ companies, 120+ thought-leadership articles ghosted.

The promise I won't bend.

Every word that leaves my desk is written by a human — by me. I don't use AI to draft, “polish,” restructure, or assist. If a sentence is in your project, it came from someone who thought about it. That is a strange thing to need to say in 2026, but it is the most important promise this practice makes.

02

Twenty years, in four notes.

  1. 2005 — 2009

    First by-lines, in print.

    Print journalism and trade publications across Manitoba. Learned to interview, edit, and meet a deadline that did not move.

  2. 2010

    The practice is founded.

    Rob Swystun Content Marketing & Ghostwriting Inc. opens for B2B clients. First articles published under client names.

  3. 2013 — present

    Ghostwriting becomes the specialty.

    Books and long-form thought-leadership become the second pillar. 120+ articles ghostwritten across industries; the first full-length book manuscript delivered in 2014.

  4. 2023 — present

    Human-written, declared in writing.

    Formal policy adopted: no AI in drafting, editing, or polishing. Every project, end to end, written by a human. The full standing policy is set out in section 04 on Home.

Wordmark and monogram

No icon. The name is the mark. A single blue period punctuates it — a writer’s full stop.

  • ConceptThe work is the mark. A simple wordmark set in Source Serif 4, with one blue period as a small signal of voice and craft.
  • ConstructionSource Serif 4, weight 400, letter-spacing −0.018em. The period is the single use of accent color in the lockup.
  • Minimum sizeWordmark: 16 px on screen, 12 mm in print. Monogram: 24 px / 16 mm.
  • Clear spaceEqual to the cap height of “R” on all sides. Never crowded by other elements.
  • Don’tsDon’t recolor the period to anything but the accent or pure ink. Don’t set the wordmark in italic, all caps, or any non-serif typeface. Don’t add a tagline lock-up directly under the wordmark.

06

Principles

From Pixel Perfect Precision · The core

Restraint over decoration.

Every gradient, glow, icon set, and rounded glass panel was left on the cutting room floor. The site reads like a well-set page: ample margins, one accent, and type doing the work that animation usually pretends to do.

From Pixel Perfect Precision · Techniques

Edges that hold their lines.

Everything sits on a 4-point baseline. Hairlines are 1px on the rule color — never on pure black. Type uses optical-size axes so display copy stays crisp and body stays readable.

From Pixel Perfect Precision · Accessibility

Real contrast, real reading.

Ink on white clears AAA at body sizes. Accent on white clears AA Large. Focus states use a 2px Ink ring — not a generic blue browser default.

From Pixel Perfect Precision · Worst case

Built for real copy.

Headlines tested at twice their length. Testimonials tested with three-line names. Cards tested with both short and long descriptions. Nothing is sized for the demo.

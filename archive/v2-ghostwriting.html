Plate ⅡFour kinds of book project

Different formats. Same craft.

If your project doesn't fit cleanly into one of these — write to me anyway. Most don't fit cleanly the first time.

  1. i.

    The business book.

    For executives and founders ready to put a methodology, philosophy, or playbook between covers. The work is half thought-leadership articulation, half storytelling — and we do both in the discovery phase.

  2. ii.

    The memoir.

    For people with a life or career worth recording — written in your cadence, not memoir-template prose. The thing nobody warns you about is that the structure is harder than the writing. We get that part right first.

  3. iii.

    Long-form thought-leadership.

    Op-eds, serialized books, deeply-reported articles that establish a point of view in your field. Often the precursor to a full book contract — and sometimes the better stand-alone result.

  4. iv.

    Selective fiction.

    Literary and genre fiction, novel-length, ghostwritten or co-written. Two slots per year, by application — fiction is a different muscle and I won't take it on unless I have the time to do it well.

Plate ⅢThe process ※ three phases

Total duration Most full-length books run between eight and fourteen months from kickoff to a submission-ready manuscript. Pacing is set by your interview availability.

From the first call to a finished manuscript.

Every project moves through three phases. Each has a clear deliverable and a clear sign-off — so you always know where the book is and where it is going next.

  1. Phase one
    Discovery

    Listen, then outline.

    We start with a paid sixty-minute consult, then a series of recorded interviews that capture your thinking, your voice, and the structure of the book. I write the outline — you sign off before any chapter is drafted.

      You receive
    • Voice & audience brief
    • Annotated outline of the full book
    • One sample chapter, drafted to the brief
  2. Phase two
    Drafting

    Draft, in your voice.

    I draft chapters one at a time, with a regular interview cadence to feed each new section. You read as we go, flag anything that doesn't sound like you, and we adjust before we move on. No surprises at the end.

      You receive
    • Chapter drafts on a set cadence
    • Voice-check review notes after each pass
    • Two revision rounds, built in to the timeline
  3. Phase three
    Finishing

    Polish, then hand it over.

    Full manuscript pass, structural edit, line edit, and proofread. You receive a final manuscript ready for your editor, agent, or self-publishing pipeline. I disappear from the acknowledgments page if you want me to.

      You receive
    • Final manuscript, DOCX and PDF
    • Front-matter draft and back-matter notes
    • Submission-ready files for editor or agent

Plate ⅣStatement of scope

What is in, and what is not.

Ghostwriting engagements are not all-purpose author services. Here is what every project includes, and what it does not — so the scope is clear before either of us signs anything.

In scope ※ every project

Always included.

  • Discovery interviews, recorded and transcribed
  • Annotated outline and chapter map
  • Full manuscript drafting — every word by Rob
  • Three rounds of revisions, in the timeline
  • Voice-check feedback loop after each chapter
  • Light structural and line editing of final draft
  • NDA and full ghosting (no by-line, no credit)
  • One real human, project-managing himself

Out of scope ※ outsourced or extra

Not included.

  • Developmental editing by a third-party editor
  • Copy-edit and proofread by a dedicated editor
  • Cover design, interior layout, typesetting
  • Agent representation or publishing strategy
  • Print fulfilment, distribution, marketing
  • Audiobook recording or narration
  • AI tooling, at any stage, ever, for any reason
  • Anyone but Rob writing your sentences

Plate ⅤCommon questions, plainly answered

Before you book, five answers.

i.

How long does a book take?
Most business books and memoirs run between eight and fourteen months from kickoff to a submission-ready manuscript. Fiction varies more. The pacing is set by interview availability — the more time you can give the discovery phase, the more accurately the book sounds like you.
ii.

What does it cost?
Ghostwriting is a significant investment — typically in the tens of thousands of dollars for a full-length book. The exact figure depends on length, complexity, and timeline. The paid consult is where we discuss numbers honestly, and the consult fee credits toward the project if we proceed.
iii.

Will it sound like me, not like you?
Yes — that is the whole job. Voice capture is the first phase for a reason. I work from recorded interviews, your existing writing, and the cadence you use when you talk. The first chapter goes through a voice review before we draft a second.
iv.

Do you use AI to help?
No — and not in any form. No drafting, no editing assistance, no “polishing,” no machine summary of your interviews. Every word in your manuscript is written by a human. This is the policy I will not bend on any project. The full standing policy is set out in Plate Ⅴ on the home page.
v.

Will your name appear anywhere?
Only if you want it to. The default is full ghosting — NDA in place, no by-line, no acknowledgments, no portfolio listing of your project. Some clients prefer a co-author credit; that is a separate conversation we have in the consult.

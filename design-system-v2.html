Wordmark and monogram

The name is the mark. A heavy sans-serif wordmark with a small warm period — a writer’s full stop and the only color in the lockup.

  • ConceptNo icon, no flourish. A bold, plain sans-serif name with a single warm period — used in place of an icon at small sizes.
  • ConstructionInter, weight 800, letter-spacing −0.04em. The period is the only color in the lockup.
  • Minimum sizeWordmark: 14 px on screen, 10 mm in print. Monogram: 20 px / 14 mm.
  • Clear spaceEqual to the cap height of “R” on all sides.
  • Don’tsDon’t use a lighter weight. Don’t color anything beyond the period. Don’t italicize. Don’t set in all caps. Don’t lock it up with a tagline directly underneath.

07

Principles

From Pixel Perfect Precision · The core

Restraint over decoration.

No gradients, no glassmorphism, no decorative icons. The page is black, the type is heavy, and the accents are used once. The space carries the rest.

From Pixel Perfect Precision · Techniques

Edges that hold their lines.

Everything sits on a 4-point baseline. Hairlines are 1px on the line color. Type tracks tighter as it grows — display copy at −0.045em, body at 0.

From Pixel Perfect Precision · Accessibility

Real contrast, real reading.

White on near-black clears AAA at body sizes. Blue on near-black clears AA Large. Yellow on near-black clears AAA. Every accent earns its place by passing the contrast check.

From Pixel Perfect Precision · Worst case

Built for real copy.

Headlines tested at twice their length. Testimonials with three-line names. Service descriptions tested with both terse and long copy. Nothing is sized for the demo.

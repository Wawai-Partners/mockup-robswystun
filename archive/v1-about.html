The long version

I've been writing professionally since 2005, when I sold my first piece of journalism while still figuring out what professional writing even meant. Twenty years later, I have a clearer answer: it means showing up with the discipline of a tradesperson and the ear of an editor, for whoever is paying you to do it well.

The ghostwriting practice.

I've been ghostwriting books and long-form thought-leadership for 13+ years. The clients are usually executives, founders, and professionals with a book's worth of expertise but no spare ten months to turn it into a manuscript. My job is to extract what they know, write it in their voice, and disappear from the acknowledgments page. Business books, memoir, the occasional novel.

The B2B content marketing practice.

On the marketing side, I've written for 90+ companies — articles, blogs, white papers, case studies, press releases, landing pages. Mostly for B2B teams in SaaS, biosciences, fintech, electric vehicles, and franchising. The work is straightforward: understand the audience, do the research, write something a real person would want to finish reading.

The thing I'm careful about.

Every word that leaves my desk is written by a human — me. I don't use AI to draft, "polish," restructure, or assist. If a sentence is in your project, it came from someone who thought about it. That's a strange thing to need to say in 2026, but it is the most important promise this practice makes.

    Business book

The process

Three phases. No surprises.

From the first call to a finished manuscript, the work moves through three phases. Each one has a clear deliverable and a clear sign-off — so you always know where the book is and where it's going next.

  1. 01 Phase oneDiscovery

    Listen, then outline.

    We start with a paid 60-minute consult, then a series of recorded interviews that capture your thinking, your voice, and the structure of the book. I write the outline — you sign off before any chapter is drafted.

    You receive
    • Voice & audience brief
    • Annotated outline
    • Sample chapter
  2. 02 Phase twoDrafting

    Draft, in your voice.

    I draft chapters one at a time, with a regular interview cadence to feed each new section. You read as we go, flag anything that doesn't sound like you, and we adjust before we move on. No surprises at the end.

    You receive
    • Chapter drafts on a set cadence
    • Voice-check review notes
    • Two revision rounds built in
  3. 03 Phase threeFinishing

    Polish, then hand it over.

    Full manuscript pass, structural edit, line edit, and proofread. You get a final manuscript ready for your editor, agent, or self-publishing pipeline. I disappear from the acknowledgments page if you want me to.

    You receive
    • Final manuscript (DOCX + PDF)
    • Front-matter draft
    • Submission-ready files

Scope

What's in, and what isn't.

Ghostwriting engagements are not all-purpose author services. Here's what every project comes with, and what it doesn't — so the scope is clear before we sign anything.

Included in every project

Always in scope

  • Discovery interviews (recorded & transcribed)
  • Annotated outline and chapter map
  • Full manuscript drafting — every word by Rob
  • Three rounds of revisions built into the timeline
  • Voice-check feedback loop after each chapter
  • Light structural and line editing of final draft
  • NDA and full ghosting (no by-line, no credit)
  • One real human, project-managing himself
Not included — outsourced or extra

Out of scope

  • Developmental editing by a third-party editor
  • Copy-editing & proofreading by a dedicated editor
  • Cover design, interior layout, typesetting
  • Agent representation or publishing strategy
  • Print fulfilment, distribution, marketing campaigns
  • Audiobook recording or narration
  • AI tooling of any kind, at any stage, ever
  • Anyone but Rob writing your sentences

Common questions

Before you book, a few answers.

Most clients arrive with the same five or six questions. Here they are with the most direct version of each answer.

How long does a book take? +
Most business books and memoirs run 8 to 14 months from kickoff to a submission-ready manuscript. Fiction varies more. The pacing is set by interview availability — the more time you can give to the discovery phase, the more accurately the book sounds like you.
What does it cost? +
Ghostwriting is a significant investment — typically in the tens of thousands of dollars for a full-length book. Exact figures depend on length, complexity, and timeline. The paid consult is where we discuss numbers honestly, and the consult fee credits toward the project if we proceed.
Will it sound like me, not like you? +
Yes — that's the whole job. Voice capture is the first phase for a reason. I work from recorded interviews, your existing writing, and the cadence you use when you talk. The first chapter goes through a voice review before we draft a second.
Do you use AI to help? +
No — and not in any form. No drafting, no editing assistance, no "polishing," no AI summarization of your interviews. Every word in your manuscript is written by a human. This is the policy I won't bend, on any project.
Will your name appear anywhere? +
Only if you want it to. The default is full ghosting — NDA in place, no by-line, no acknowledgments, no portfolio listing of your project. Some clients prefer a co-author credit; that's a separate conversation we have in the consult.
